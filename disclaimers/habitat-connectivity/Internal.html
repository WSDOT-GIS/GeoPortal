Habitat Connectivity Support Structures provides spatial information on structures built on or along the state highway system to benefit wildlife, usually by providing safe opportunities for wildlife to move past a highway or to reduce collisions. These include bridges, culverts, wildlife barrier fencing, jumpout ramps, and wildlife guards. It also includes structures that were never intended to benefit wildlife but, as a result of monitoring with motion-triggered cameras, have been shown to be providing significant safe wildlife passage.
